Barra do Turvo

Barra do Turvo is a municipality in the state of São Paulo in Brazil . The population is 7,828 (2015 est.) in an area of 1008 km². [1] The elevation is 158 m.

Conservation

The municipality contains part of the 182,596 hectares (451,200 acres) Rio Turvo State Park , created in 2008. [2] It contains 97% of the 2,722 hectares (6,730 acres) Planalto do Turvo Environmental Protection Area , created at the same time. [3] It contains the 3,235 hectares (7,990 acres) Rio Pardinho e Rio Vermelho Environmental Protection Area . [4] It contains part of the 40,175 hectares (99,270 acres) Caverna do Diabo State Park , also created in 2008. [5] The municipality contains 3% of the 64,625 hectares (159,690 acres) Quilombos do Médio Ribeira Environmental Protection Area , established in 2008. [6] It contains the 1,530 hectares (3,800 acres) Pinheirinhos Sustainable Development Reserve , also created in 2008. [7] It contains the 3,175 hectares (7,850 acres) Barreiro/Anhemas Sustainable Development Reserve , created at the same time. [8]
